5/12/2025 A questionable start

First post.

During a Game Development course, my instructor once told us "Make a portfolio to document your work." This was followed by the very enthusiastic digital hand of the student next to me as he proceeded to give us a full tour of his website. A blog spanning 5 years of his life working on these mobile games, impressive right? Well, that seemed the general consensus on the zoom call. But now I can't help but wonder if he ever got recognition for such an impressive feat, the suspiciously similar “Zenith jump” to flappy bird, or that one weird game with the plastic man with the stretchy face. Though these games wouldn't get him far in popularity considering the cut throat world programming, he had a window to where he was and what he’s achieved now. This is also, for any hesitant game makers out there, exactly what you should be doing when learning Then our instructor cut him off so we could get the hell on with learning C#. Before writing this first blog post, I thought, "Oh god, I really don't want to code a blog post".
Because I don't really anything impressive to document, nothing too big happens in my life, the usual things of though, and my art, so that's what I've settled on. So now begs the question: how on earth am I going to consolidate all the poorly executed projects I've made and or will make into an HTML script I can barely read? Then I realised, no one's reading this. And if you are... welcome to this strange site. If I'm being honest, this terrible first impression I'm making is partly due to the fact I detest writers block, and after avoiding writing anything for two weeks, I thought I might as well write SOMETHING just so I have solid proof that yes- I did make a website. I was here
